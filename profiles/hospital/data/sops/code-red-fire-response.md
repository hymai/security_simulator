# SOP: Code Red Response (Fire in a Patient Care Area)

Applies when smoke or heat is detected, or a fire is reported, in a patient
care unit.

## Step 1 — RACE: Rescue, Alarm, Contain

- **Charge Nurse**: Rescue any patient in immediate danger from the room of
  origin.
- **Charge Nurse**: Activate the nearest pull station and announce "Code Red"
  with the unit location overhead.
- **Unit Staff**: Close all doors on the affected unit to contain smoke, starting
  with the room of origin.

## Step 2 — Staged evacuation (defend-in-place then relocate)

- **Charge Nurse**: Move patients in the fire compartment horizontally through
  the fire doors into the adjacent smoke compartment first.
- **House Supervisor**: Direct full evacuation to the floor below only if the
  fire service or fire panel indicates the smoke compartment is compromised.
- **Unit Staff**: Account for every patient and staff member against the unit
  census at the relocation point.

## Step 3 — Support to fire service and all-clear

- **Security Officer**: Meet the fire service at the designated entrance and
  escort them to the unit.
- **House Supervisor**: Provide the fire service with the unit census and
  confirm no patients are unaccounted for.
- **Charge Nurse**: Do not permit patient return to the unit until the fire
  service declares it safe.
