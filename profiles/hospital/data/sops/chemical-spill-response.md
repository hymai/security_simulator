# SOP: Chemical Spill Response (Laboratory)

Applies when a reagent or cleaning chemical spills in the Pathology Lab,
releasing fumes or creating a contact hazard.

## Step 1 — Evacuate and contain

- **Lab Technician**: Evacuate the immediate area and close the lab door to
  contain fumes.
- **Lab Technician**: Activate the fume-hood alarm or call the lab supervisor
  if not already activated.
- **Security Officer**: Restrict access to the lab corridor and hold the
  elevator bank on that floor away from the spill floor.

## Step 2 — Assessment and cleanup

- **Lab Supervisor**: Identify the chemical from the Safety Data Sheet (SDS)
  and determine whether Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) response is
  required.
- **EHS Officer**: Don appropriate PPE and contain/neutralize the spill per the
  SDS, or escalate to the hazmat team if beyond staff capability.

## Step 3 — Clearance and reporting

- **EHS Officer**: Air-monitor the area and declare it safe for re-entry.
- **Lab Supervisor**: Confirm no staff or patients were exposed, and refer any
  exposed individual to Occupational Health.
- **Lab Supervisor**: File the chemical spill incident report.
