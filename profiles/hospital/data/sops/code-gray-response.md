# SOP: Code Gray Response (Combative or Violent Person)

Applies when a patient, visitor, or family member becomes physically
aggressive toward staff or other patients.

## Step 1 — Alert and initial de-escalation

- **Charge Nurse**: Announce "Code Gray" with the unit location overhead or via
  the panic button.
- **Primary Nurse**: Attempt verbal de-escalation from a safe distance; do not
  place yourself between the individual and the exit.
- **Security Officer**: Respond immediately to the announced location.

## Step 2 — Containment and safety

- **Security Officer**: Position to prevent the individual from reaching other
  patients or the exit while maintaining a safe distance.
- **Charge Nurse**: Clear other patients and non-essential staff from the
  immediate area.
- **House Supervisor**: Determine whether restraint or sedation is clinically
  indicated and, if so, direct the physician to order it.

## Step 3 — Resolution and reporting

- **Security Officer**: Confirm the area is safe and stand down once the
  individual is calm, restrained, or removed.
- **Charge Nurse**: Document the incident timeline and any injuries in the
  medical record.
- **House Supervisor**: File the workplace violence incident report within 24
  hours.
