# Threat Catalog — Riverside General Hospital (synthetic)

This catalog lists threats the hospital trains against. It is reference
material for scenario generation only. It contains no response procedures.

## Workplace Violence threats

### Code Gray — combative or violent person

A patient, visitor, or family member becomes physically aggressive toward
staff or other patients, most commonly in the Emergency Department waiting
area or on an inpatient behavioral health unit. Triggers include intoxication,
psychiatric crisis, or a dispute over care decisions. The individual may
threaten staff, throw objects, or attempt to leave against medical advice
while combative.

## Facilities Management threats

### Code Red — fire in a patient care area

Smoke or heat is detected in a patient care unit (most commonly the 3rd Floor
Medical/Surgical unit). Because patients may be non-ambulatory, evacuation is
staged (horizontal defend-in-place before full evacuation) rather than an
immediate full-building evacuation. Occupancy on the 3rd Floor during a shift
is up to 30 patients and 15 staff.

## Hazardous Materials threats

### Chemical spill in the laboratory

A reagent or cleaning chemical spills in the Pathology Lab, releasing fumes or
creating a slip/contact hazard. Indicators include a strong chemical odor,
visible spill, or an activated fume-hood alarm. Staff and adjacent-area
occupants may be exposed before containment.
