# SOP: Bomb threat and suspicious item

Aligned with SGSecure guidance: treat every threat as credible until the
Police assess otherwise. Two entry points: a threat received by phone or in
writing, and a suspicious item found in or around the building.

## A — Threat call received

1. **Call taker (management office or tenant reception)**: Stay calm and
   keep the caller talking. Record on the bomb-threat checklist: exact
   words, stated device location and detonation time, reason given, and
   background sounds, accent, and apparent age of the caller. Do not hang up
   even after the caller does — leave the line open.
2. **Call taker**: Immediately after the call, notify the FCC on the
   emergency extension with the completed checklist details. Do not discuss
   the call with colleagues — rumour spreads faster than instructions.
3. **FCC Security Officer**: Call the Police at 999, relay the checklist
   details, and inform the Fire Safety Manager and Site Main Controller.
4. **Site Main Controller**: Direct a search of the stated location (if any)
   and the building's publicly accessible areas — lobby, lift lobbies,
   toilets, carpark, loading bay, refuse areas — by Security Officers and
   CERT members in pairs, searching areas they know well enough to spot
   anything out of place. Radios and mobile phones are NOT used within 25 m
   of any suspect item.

## B — Suspicious item found

5. **Finder / Security Officer**: Do not touch, move, open, or cover the
   item. Note its exact location and description, withdraw at least 25 m
   (100 m for anything vehicle-sized), and report to the FCC from a distance
   — by fixed line or in person, not by radio near the item.
6. **FCC Security Officer**: Call the Police at 999 with the item's
   location and description, and pull up the CCTV recording of the area to
   identify who left the item and when. Preserve the recording.
7. **Site Incident Controller**: Cordon the area with CERT members at least
   25 m out, clear occupants from the cordoned area, and hold the routes the
   Police will use to approach.
8. **Fire Safety Manager**: Decide, with the Police's advice, between
   evacuation and in-place protection. If evacuating, route occupants AWAY
   from the item's location — amend the standard staircase and assembly
   routing if the item sits on the normal route, and announce the amended
   route clearly over the PA system.
9. **Site Main Controller**: On Police arrival, hand over: the checklist or
   item description, CCTV findings, cordon extent, and the building's
   floor plans from the FCC document set. Re-entry and stand-down happen
   only on the Police's clearance, announced over the PA system.
