# SOP: In-place protection

Executes the in-place protection component of the ERP. Used when keeping
occupants inside protects them better than evacuating — for example external
smoke or fumes near the building, a security incident in the surrounding
streets, or a hazmat release outdoors or in the basements while upper floors
are unaffected.

1. **Fire Safety Manager**: Decide in-place protection (rather than
   evacuation) based on the location of the hazard, and record the decision
   and time. If the hazard is inside occupied areas of the tower, this SOP
   does not apply — evacuate instead.
2. **FCC Security Officer**: Announce over the PA/EVAC system that occupants
   are to remain on their floors, stay away from the affected area, and
   await further instructions. Repeat the announcement every 10 minutes.
3. **FCC Security Officer**: Shut down the fresh-air intakes and the AHUs
   serving affected zones so external smoke or fumes are not drawn into
   occupied floors.
4. **Security Officers (ground)**: Close and secure the main lobby doors,
   loading bay shutter and carpark entrances; permit exit but no entry, and
   redirect arriving visitors away from the building.
5. **Site Main Controller**: Inform SCDF (995) or Police (999) as
   appropriate of the in-place protection decision, the reason, and the
   estimated number of occupants sheltering.
6. **CERT response team members**: On their assigned floors, keep occupants
   away from the windows on the affected side, close doors to staircases,
   and report the floor's status to the Site Incident Controller every 15
   minutes.
7. **Fire Safety Manager**: Stand down in-place protection only when the
   external agency confirms the hazard has cleared, then announce resumption
   of normal operations over the PA system and log the end time.
