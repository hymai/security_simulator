# SOP: Power failure and lift entrapment

Applies to loss of incoming supply, genset problems, and occupants trapped
in lifts. Lift rescue is performed only by the lift contractor's technician
or SCDF — never by security or CERT members forcing doors.

## Power failure

1. **FCC Security Officer**: Confirm on the FCC panels which supplies are
   lost and whether the standby generator has started and picked up the
   essential-services load (exit lighting, fire pumps, one lift per bank,
   FCC systems). Log the time.
2. **FCC Security Officer**: Announce over the PA system that the building
   is on standby power, normal lifts are out of service, and occupants
   should remain on their floors unless instructed otherwise.
3. **Building Technician**: Check the genset room (basement 2): confirm the
   set is running within limits and note the day-tank level — report the
   remaining runtime to the Site Main Controller every 30 minutes.
4. **Site Main Controller**: Notify the building's electrical contractor and
   SP Group's fault line of the outage, and obtain an estimated restoration
   time. If the genset has failed to start, treat the building as having no
   fire pump capability: the Fire Safety Manager must decide whether
   occupancy can continue or the building should be progressively cleared.
5. **Security Officers**: Post at the main lobby and carpark entrances to
   manage access — speed gates and card readers may be offline; verify
   passes visually and log entries manually.

## Lift entrapment

6. **FCC Security Officer**: On an intercom call or trapped-passenger alarm
   from a lift car, establish which car and between which floors it has
   stopped (lift supervisory panel), reassure the occupants that help is
   coming, and stay in contact every few minutes. Ask whether anyone inside
   is unwell.
7. **FCC Security Officer**: Call the lift contractor's 24-hour rescue line
   and log the call time. If anyone in the car is unwell, distressed, or
   the contractor cannot arrive within 30 minutes, call SCDF at 995 for
   rescue.
8. **CERT response team member**: Wait at the landing nearest the stalled
   car to meet the technician or SCDF, keep the lift lobby clear, and relay
   the occupants' condition between the car intercom (via the FCC) and the
   rescuers.
9. **Site Main Controller**: After any entrapment rescue, take the affected
   lift out of service until the contractor certifies it, and record the
   incident (car, duration, occupants, any injuries) for the building's
   incident log.
