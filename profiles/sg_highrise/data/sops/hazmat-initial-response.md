# SOP: Hazmat initial response

Applies to chemical spills, refrigerant leaks and unidentified fumes in the
building — most likely at the loading bay (basement 1) or the chiller plant
(basement 2). CERT provides the initial response before SCDF arrival; entry
into a contaminated space is SCDF's job, not CERT's.

1. **First responder on scene (Security Officer or CERT member)**: Withdraw
   upwind/uphill of the release, keep others out, and report to the FCC by
   radio: location, what is leaking or spilled (drum labels if readable from
   a safe distance), approximate quantity, and any casualties. Do not touch
   the material and do not switch electrical equipment on or off inside the
   affected area.
2. **FCC Security Officer**: Call SCDF at 995 and report a hazmat incident
   with the substance information received. Inform the Fire Safety Manager
   and Site Main Controller.
3. **Site Incident Controller**: Establish a cordon at least 25 m from the
   release (further for strong fumes), using the CERT response team to hold
   the basement lift lobbies and staircase doors on the affected level.
   Nobody enters the cordon before SCDF arrives — including for rescue,
   unless a casualty is visible and reachable without entering the
   contaminated atmosphere.
4. **FCC Security Officer**: For a basement release, shut down the AHUs and
   fresh-air intakes that could draw fumes to occupied floors, and switch
   the carpark ventilation to extraction mode.
5. **Chiller plant cases — Building Technician**: If the release is in the
   chiller plant, confirm from the plant room's gas-detection panel (outside
   the door) whether the refrigerant alarm has activated, and isolate the
   plant remotely. Never enter the plant room to check on a person —
   report a suspected casualty to the FCC for SCDF's rescue.
6. **Loading bay cases — Security Officer (loading bay)**: Stop all further
   deliveries, move vehicles clear of the dock if drivers are with them, and
   obtain the delivery's chemical documentation (Safety Data Sheet) from the
   driver or consignment note for hand-over to SCDF.
7. **Fire Safety Manager**: Decide whether affected basement levels or the
   floors above need evacuation or in-place protection, and execute the
   relevant SOP.
8. **Site Main Controller**: On SCDF arrival, hand over: substance
   information and SDS, location and cordon extent, ventilation status, and
   any unaccounted-for persons.
