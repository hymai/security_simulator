# Fire and hazmat threat catalog

## Fire threats

- **Refuse chute / bin centre fire (basement 1).** Discarded smouldering
  material or deliberate ignition in the bin centre. Smoke spreads to the
  loading bay and can migrate up the chute. History: two minor bin-centre
  fires in Singapore commercial buildings in the past year were suspected
  arson; the bin centre CCTV has a blind corner behind the compactor.
- **Carpark vehicle fire (basements 1–4).** Vehicle or EV charging-point
  fire; heavy black smoke fills the deck quickly, triggering multiple
  detectors; the carpark extraction system slows but does not stop spread.
- **Electrical riser fire.** Overheated busbar or cable joint in a riser;
  smoke appears on several floors near the service core at once because the
  riser is stacked; detectors on multiple floors can activate in sequence.
- **F&B podium kitchen fire (levels 1–3).** Oil fire on a cooking line
  spreading to the kitchen exhaust duct; duct fires can travel between floors
  of the podium; sprinklers cover the dining areas but the duct interior
  relies on the duct's own suppression.
- **Tenant floor fire during off-hours.** Small fires (pantry appliances,
  overloaded power strips) on largely empty floors, detected first by a
  single smoke detector; risk of delayed discovery and of smoke spreading
  through an AHU left running.

## Hazmat threats

- **Refrigerant leak in the chiller plant (basement 2).** R-134a release
  displaces oxygen in the enclosed plant room; a technician inside can be
  incapacitated with little warning; the gas is odourless and heavier than
  air, so it pools in the basement.
- **Chemical spill at the loading bay (basement 1).** Cleaning-contractor or
  F&B chemical drums (degreasers, chlorine-based sanitisers) dropped or
  punctured during delivery; mixing of incompatible chemicals produces
  irritant fumes that spread to the carpark and service lift lobby.
- **Fumes drawn into occupied floors.** Basement fumes or external smoke
  entering the fresh-air intakes and being distributed by the AHUs to
  occupied floors before anyone at ground level notices a source.
