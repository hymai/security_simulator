# Security and utilities threat catalog

## Bomb threat and suspicious items

- **Bomb threat phone call.** A call to the building management office or a
  tenant reception claiming a device in the building, sometimes with a
  stated detonation time or location, sometimes vague. Most are hoaxes, but
  Singapore's threat posture (SGSecure) treats every call as credible until
  assessed otherwise.
- **Suspicious package or unattended bag.** An unattended bag in the main
  lobby, a lift lobby, or the carpark; or a delivered package with wires,
  stains, excessive tape, no return address, or an unusual smell noticed at
  the mailroom or loading bay.
- **Threatening note or email.** A written threat against the building or a
  specific tenant, discovered by a tenant or cleaner.

## Intrusion and unauthorised access

- **After-hours intrusion.** Forced or tailgated entry through the carpark
  or loading bay outside office hours; motion alarms in tenant space; a
  person moving between floors via the exit staircases to avoid lift CCTV.
- **Hostile reconnaissance.** A person photographing security posts, speed
  gates, the FCC door, or loading-bay schedules; repeat visits with no
  legitimate business.

## Utilities failure

- **Power failure.** Loss of incoming supply to the building; standby
  generator starts for essential services only — normal lifts stop, tenant
  floors lose general lighting and power; if the genset fails to start or
  its day tank runs down, essential services are also lost.
- **Lift entrapment.** Occupants trapped in a lift car between floors during
  a power dip or lift fault; car intercom connects to the FCC; entrapments
  during a power failure can occur in several cars at once.
- **Water leak into an electrical riser.** A burst pipe or AHU condensate
  overflow entering a riser; risk of tripping floor distribution boards and
  of localised electrical fire; affected floors may need isolation.
