# Threat Catalog — Meridian Power Substation (synthetic)

This catalog lists threats the site trains against. It is reference material for
scenario generation only. It contains no response procedures.

## Physical Security threats

### Unauthorized perimeter entry
An intruder attempts to cross the outer perimeter fence, most commonly along the
Zone 3 fence line where tree cover reduces natural surveillance. Motives range
from theft of copper and equipment to sabotage of transmission assets. Intruders
may cut, climb, or lift the fence fabric, and may use a diversion elsewhere on
the perimeter to draw officers away from the true entry point.

### Tailgating at controlled doors
An unauthorized person follows an authorized cardholder through an access-
controlled door into Block A or Block B before it closes.

## Cyber Security threats

### Intrusion into the control network
An attacker gains a foothold on the SCADA/OT network, typically via a phished
engineering workstation or an exposed remote-access service. Indicators include
anomalous outbound connections from an engineering workstation and unexpected
configuration changes on a programmable logic controller (PLC).

## Facilities Management threats

### Fire in an occupied building
A fire develops in Block B (control room and offices). Smoke and heat detectors
in Block B are the primary means of early detection. Occupancy during working
hours is up to 40 people.
