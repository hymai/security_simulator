# Site Security Measures — Meridian Power Substation (synthetic)

Reference material for scenario generation. Describes the site layout and which
security systems exist, so a generated scenario can mention the systems a threat
would trigger. It does NOT describe how staff respond — that lives in the SOP
corpus and must never appear in a scenario.

## Layout and zones
- **Zone 1** — outer public boundary and vehicle approach.
- **Zone 2** — inner compound, Block A (plant) and Block B (control room/offices).
- **Zone 3** — high-voltage yard, enclosed by a 2.4 m palisade perimeter fence.
  The Zone 3 fence line runs behind tree cover and is the site's most exposed
  approach.

## Detection and surveillance systems
- **Perimeter Intrusion Detection System (PIDS)** on the Zone 3 fence: fence-
  mounted vibration sensors that raise a zoned intrusion alarm in the Security
  Control Centre (SCC).
- **CCTV**: pan-tilt-zoom cameras cover each perimeter zone, including a dedicated
  camera on the Zone 3 fence line, plus fixed cameras in Blocks A and B.
- **Fire detection**: addressable smoke and heat detectors throughout Block B,
  annunciating at the SCC and at the Block B fire panel.
- **Access control**: card readers on all external doors and on the Zone 3 gate.

## Command and roles
- **Security Control Centre (SCC)** staffed 24/7 by an **SCC Operator** who
  monitors PIDS, CCTV, fire, and access-control alarms and coordinates by radio.
- **Security Officers** patrol Zones 1–3 and respond on foot.
- **Fire Wardens** and a **Duty Manager** are designated for building emergencies.

## Layered defense concept
Defense is layered: perimeter detection (PIDS) → surveillance (CCTV) → delay
(fence, locked gates) → response (officers). A capable adversary may defeat one
layer, so detection at the perimeter is intended to buy response time, not to
stop entry by itself.
